> Lucas Meldrum, https://lucasmeldrum.github.io/CART253/topics/assignments/mod-jam

Lucas' version of *frogfrogfrog* was so complex! I loved how the game unfolded through three different stages and that the fly could actually fight back. The boss fly was a fun surprise (and how it later summoned fly minions too) and it was nerve-wracking to see it turn redder the angrier / more damaged it got! Using a jar of flies as the scoring system was such a creative idea, and the animated movement really made it seem like the flies were bouncing against the glass, fitting perfectly with the "fly revolt against frog injustice" theme. 

I also liked the moral aspect in the dialogue that popped up when eating the first few flies, acting as a brutal (30+ point) reminder that actions really do have consequences. The drastic change in music was also effective as it made the conflict feel much more intense as it transitioned from soft creek sounds to something more chaotic and layered. 

The code was so nicely organized, named, and described! The script for the play / restart button was especially satisfying to read with all the variables and operators, it must've been tricky to figure all that math out!
